# Run-2 Winner — V3 comic-pop

**Selected** : `iterations/V3-comic-pop/`

## Why over the others

| Var | Axis | Strength | Why not winner |
|---|---|---|---|
| V1 failure-first | hook | Strong emotional pattern-interrupt @ 0–1.5s | Risky on passive testing (70% damage open ⇒ short runs); axis already explored in run-1 (cold-open) |
| V2 bullseye-target | mechanic | Adds clarity + juicy combo loop, genuine UX lift | Effect mostly visible mid-run, doesn't differentiate the *thumbnail* in a feed |
| **V3 comic-pop** | **palette** | **Thumb-stop maximal in TikTok/IG feed; genre-bending mobile-game → comics; pipeline-creativity demo** | **— winner —** |
| V4 loot-chest | endcard | Strong data-backed CTA pull (single arrow + 💎 counter Zeigarnik) | Effect lives only on endcard (last 2-3s) — first-impression in feed unchanged |

## Why thumb-stop dominates

In a feed-first ad placement (TikTok/IG/Reels), CPM is brutal and the user decides in ~200ms whether to keep watching. A radically different visual language (halftone + KAPOW comic-book) wins that 200ms regardless of whether the gameplay underneath is identical. V1/V2/V4 lift the *retention* and *CTA* halves of the funnel — V3 lifts the *impression* half, which sits upstream and gates everything else.

## Hackathon framing

Track-2 deliverable is **the pipeline / tool**, not a polished playable. The winner is the variation whose existence best argues "this pipeline produces non-trivial creative range." V3 demonstrates the pipeline can take a polished combat-loop and apply a genre-bending reskin without breaking gameplay — the strongest narrative for the demo.
