## ✅ Matches well
*   **Core concept:** The game's premise of moving castles on caterpillar tracks battling each other is accurately portrayed.
*   **Character Roster:** The inclusion of distinct monster types inside the castle (Skeletons with blasters, Cyclops with cannons, Goblins) placed on wooden platforms.
*   **UI Layout:** The fundamental HUD placement is correct (Blue health bar on top-left, Red health bar on top-right, central VS indicator).
*   **Damage feedback:** The castle correctly shakes to indicate taking damage before the layout shifts inside.

## ❌ Discrepancies
*   **[P0] Missing projectile physics vs instant hit:** The source game utilizes trajectory arcs and physical projectiles with travel time (e.g., bombs and missiles seen in `clip2_0046.png` and `clip2_0181.png`). The playable uses an instantaneous "hitscan" calculation that kills the target the moment the user lifts their finger, with no projectile rendered (video 00:15).
*   **[P0] Broken view transition / Interior layout:** In the source, the interior view is a localized cutout of the existing castle, leaving the shape, tracks, and exterior sky visible (`clip2_0023.png`). Inside the playable (00:07), the scene completely replaces the castle with a full-screen vertical brick wall structure, destroying the spatial context.
*   **[P1] Visual Bug - Damaged Sprite Bounding Box:** When the left castle receives damage in the playable, it shakes and clearly reveals a solid light-blue rectangular background representing the image's opaque bounding box, which masks out the background hills (video 00:04 - 00:06). The source utilizes properly transparent alpha layers (`clip2_0046.png`).
*   **[P1] Incorrect Initial Assets (Roof Color):** The playable starts with red roofs on the left castle (00:00) and then magically snaps to the correct blue roofs upon taking damage (00:02). The source game properly initializes the left-side player castle with blue roofs from the very beginning (`clip2_0001.png`).
*   **[P1] Aiming Visuals & Cursor Mismatch:** The source uses a shaded V-shaped trajectory cone containing circular dots, navigated by a detailed 3D finger rendering with a glowing touch tip (`clip2_0023.png`). The playable uses a thin, linear dotted line and a simplistic flat white vector hand icon (video 00:11).
*   **[P2] Background Environment Fidelity:** The source game features a rich, multi-layered environment including rain, stylized clouds, background forests, and textured terrain layers (`clip2_0001.png`). The playable utilizes highly simplified, flat vector hills and a solid brown ground block (video 00:00).
*   **[P2] UI Styling:** The source UI top bars feature thick black stylized strokes, a chunky custom "VS" logo, and castle icons below the bars (`clip2_0001.png`). The playable uses generic color gradients, thin primitive borders, and plain Arial/sans-serif text (video 00:00).

Draw the exterior castle base, trace an interior shape path, and use Canvas2D's `globalCompositeOperation = 'destination-out'` to mask away the center wall layer. 4.  **Implement basic Projectile tweening:** Do not execute `target.kill()` directly on release. On pointer up, instantiate a cannonball sprite (`ctx.arc`, fill `#000000`, `radius=4px`) and tween it along an interpolated arc to the target's `x/y` coordinates over `~400ms` before triggering damage.
