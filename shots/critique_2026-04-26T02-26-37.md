## ✅ Matches well
* **Core Theme & Assets:** The fundamental concept (2D profile battle between mobile tank-castles) translates correctly. The exterior structural shapes (towers, roofs, arches) closely resemble the source.
* **Character Art:** The character sprites used internally (Skeleton with blue gun, Red Cyclops, Green Goblin) are highly accurate to the original game's visual style.
* **Camera/Perspective Shift:** The ad effectively replicates the transition from viewing the external castle facade to the internal cut-away perspective showing unit placements. 

## ❌ Discrepancies
* **[P0] Missing core interactive gameplay** — The playable ad behaves entirely like a passive video auto-playing to the end-card. It completely omits the core drag-to-aim trajectory shooting mechanic shown via the finger-pointer in the game. *(Playable 00:00-00:20 vs. Source clip2_0023.png & clip2_0068.png)*
* **[P1] Top HUD styling is oversimplified** — The health bars, percentage texts, and "VS" text lack the chunky, stylized layout. The percentages sit crammed at the top instead of large and below the icons, and the "VS" lacks tilt and emphasis. *(Playable 00:00 vs. Source clip2_0001.png)*
* **[P1] Interior room backgrounds are too bright** — When cutting away the castle to show the units inside, the background directly behind the characters renders as a bright grey brick texture. In the source, the unlit interior room cavities are rendered as pitch black. *(Playable 00:07 vs. Source clip2_0023.png)*
* **[P2] Projectile substitution** — The playable ad uses a standard horizontal gray missile with simple gray circular smoke puffs. The original game utilizes thematic black crows carrying bombs with curling, darker smoke trails. *(Playable 00:01 vs. Source clip2_0046.png)*
* **[P2] Environment and track details missing** — The background environments are flat vectors lacking the atmospheric depth, clouds, and tree silhouettes. The tank tracks lack the internal metal gears and spiked treads of the source material. *(Playable 00:02 vs. Source clip2_0001.png)*

## 🔧 Fix list (ordered by impact)
1. **Implement Trajectory Gameplay:** Add standard DOM pointer event listeners (`pointerdown`, `pointermove`, `pointerup`) over the interior characters. On drag, use a Canvas2D path to render a dotted trajectory line (draw `Math.PI * 2` arcs, radius `~4px`, `fillStyle = '#FFFFFF'`, spaced `~15-20px` apart) to allow the player to aim, replicating the original interaction loop before the end card triggers.
2. **Revamp Top UI Elements:**
   - **VS Text:** Increase `font-size` massively, apply `color: #FFFFFF`, add `lineWidth: 3` (or `4`) `strokeStyle = '#000000'`, and mathematically rotate the canvas context by approx `-10deg`. Overlap it in the direct center over the red/blue boundary.
   - **Health Text:** Change font to a thicker "bubbly" / impact-style font. Move the vertically (`translateY`) by roughly `+35px` so the "100%" text sits *underneath* the mini-castle icons, not beside/above them. Add the heavy black stroke.
3. **Correct Interior Contrast:** When rendering the inside of the castle cutaway, apply `fillStyle = '#000000'` mapped to the bounding boxes of the room cavaties behind the characters to create stark contrast, rather than repeating the dark-grey brick asset everywhere.
4. **Environment Depth & Colors:** Update the Canvas clear/sky color to a more green-tinted teal (approx `#c7e0c4`). Add a parallax background array layer filled with dark green (`#4d8266`) vector silhouettes of rounded trees to give the rolling hills depth.
