## ✅ Matches well
*   **Core concept:** Two multi-story moving castles mounted on tank tracks fighting head-to-head.
*   **UI layout:** The general placement of the top UI elements, including the team-colored health bars, "VS", and percentage labels.
*   **Interior Cutaway:** The mechanic of zooming into the castle interior to view floors containing units (Skeleton, Red cyclops, Green orc) placed on wooden platforms.
*   **Basic interaction loop:** Aiming manually via touch/drag originating from a character inside the castle to generate a shot trajectory.

## ❌ Discrepancies
*   **[P0] Missing mechanics for Aiming Slingshot UI** — Video `00:07` vs Source `clip2_0023`. The source game uses a pull-back slingshot UI with two solid tension bands forming a "V" shape connected to the user's touch. The playable removes this completely, acting as a simple drag-forward curved line.
*   **[P0] Lack of physical destruction & debris** — Video `00:05` vs Source `clip2_0158`. The source features massive physics-based destruction where chunks of the castle break apart accompanied by floating damage text (`-140`). The playable simply shakes the intact structure and slaps a flat, brown transparent circle sprite on heavily damaged areas.
*   **[P0] Incorrect spatial orientation in cutaway view** — Video `00:07` vs Source `clip2_0023`. In the source, the castle is traversing a hill, so the interior view and its wooden floors are tilted at roughly a 15-20 degree angle. The playable awkwardly cuts to a perfectly vertical, flat orientation, breaking positional continuity.
*   **[P1] Overly simplistic flat environment** — Video `00:00` vs Source `clip2_0001`. The source has a rich, multi-layered parallax background (clouds, various depths of jungle foliage, atmospheric rain). The playable relies on completely flat, untextured vector hills.
*   **[P1] Trajectory visualization** — Video `00:07` vs Source `clip2_0068`. The source trajectory is composed of distinct, large white circles with black outlines. The playable uses a thin, native dashed line. 
*   **[P1] Projectile mismatch** — Video `00:01` vs Source `clip2_0046`. The playable fires generic floating bomb sprites. The source utilizes crows/ravens carrying the bombs to the target.
*   **[P1] UI Typography & Styling** — Video `00:00` vs Source `clip2_0001`. The source UI features thick, bold, blocky fonts with strong black outlines (no border bounding boxes on the health bars itself) and a tilted `VS`. The playable uses thin, basic fonts trapped inside rigid rectangular borders.
*   **[P2] Team colors absent on top UI icons** — Video `00:00` vs Source `clip2_0001`. The top-left and top-right castle icons in the source have Blue and Red roofs respectively to match the health bars. The playable uses identical grey icons for both sides.

## 🔧 Fix list (ordered by impact)
1.  **Rebuild Aiming Slingshot & Trajectory UI (Canvas2D):** Implement the tension bands. During `pointerMove`, draw two `lineTo` lines starting from the character's anchor points converging at the touch coordinates. Replace the native `setLineDash` trajectory with a loop rendering individual `arc()` circles (approx radius `4px`, `fillStyle: '#FFFFFF'`, `lineWidth: 2`, `strokeStyle: '#000000'`) calculating path via `Math.sin`/`Math.cos` velocity arcs.
2.  **Correct Interior Rotation Angle:** Read the rotation state of the castle exterior when triggering the cutaway. Apply a CSS transform `transform: rotate(-18deg)` or Canvas context rotation `ctx.rotate(-18 * Math.PI / 180)` to the interior view container so the inner platforms match the physics tilt of the outside world.
3.  **Upgrade UI Styling (HTML/CSS):**
    *   Remove the surrounding borders from the health containers (`border: none`).
    *   Adjust left health bar base color to `#0082FF` and right to `#FF0000`.
    *   Update the percentage text style: `font-size: 28px; font-weight: 900; color: #FFFFFF; -webkit-text-stroke: 2px #000000;`.
    *   Replace the grey roof pixels on the UI icons to `#5681C3` (Left) and `#C5443F` (Right).
4.  **Enhance Background Depth Parallax:** Break the flat background into at least 3 separate scrolling layers. Use a sky color `#C2D2B2`, midground tree layer `#81A077`, and textured foreground dirt `#683424` to replicate the feeling of depth in the source frames. 
5.  **Remove Flat Damage Overlay:** Delete the brown fuzz overlay (`00:05`). To fake destruction cheaply without full physics, implement an animated particle burst object array (`requestAnimationFrame`) paired with a reverse Canvas `globalCompositeOperation = 'destination-out'` mask to punch visual holes in the castle sprite where the impact struck.
