## ❌ Discrepancies
*   **[P0] Castle physics and terrain:** In the source, castles are mobile units driving on treads over uneven hilly terrain, causing the entire structure (and interior) to tilt dynamically (`clip2_0046.png`). In the playable, the castles are perfectly vertical, stationary, and embedded into a flat ground surface (00:00, 00:03).
*   **[P1] Environment art style:** The source uses a lush, vibrant green jungle/forest background with organic foreground topography (`clip2_0001.png`). The playable uses a flat, blueish-grey winter/pine theme (00:00).
*   **[P1] Interior camera transition:** The source frames show the camera dynamically zooming into the actual tilted exterior silhouette of the castle for the interior phase (`clip2_0023.png`). The playable abruptly overlays a perfectly vertical, boxy split-screen projection with a solid green background on the left side (00:03).
*   **[P1] Character weapons & details:** The source characters explicitly hold and fire distinct weapons (red has a cannon, skull has a minigun, green has a bazooka) (`clip2_0068.png`). The playable characters are flat, untextured sprites holding no weapons at all, shooting generic projectiles entirely from their core (00:04).
*   **[P1] Castle texture and assets:** The source castles feature highly detailed stone brick textures, wooden foundations, and massive tank treads (`clip2_0001.png`). The playable castles are simple, flat-colored vector shapes (00:00).
*   **[P2] Top UI styling:** The source uses a customized layout with a prominent stylized overlapping "VS" text graphic and embedded castle icons (`clip2_0001.png`). The playable uses standard straight-edged colored bars with a basic black `#000000` "VS" box (00:00).
*   **[P2] Bottom UI inclusion:** The playable features three large character portrait buttons pinned to the bottom of the screen during the interior phase (00:04). These are completely absent in the source reference frames (`clip2_0023.png`).
